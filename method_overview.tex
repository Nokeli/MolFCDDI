\subsection{Method Overview}

We propose a DDI prediction framework that combines a pretrained MolFCL backbone with an explicit cross-drug fragment interaction head. Given a drug pair, a shared MolFCL encoder first extracts fragment-level representations and prompt-enhanced global molecular representations for each drug. A directional Cross-Fragment Interaction module is then applied to model how fragments from one drug attend to and interact with fragments from the other drug, yielding two directional interaction summaries together with a pair-level fragment interaction representation. The directional summaries are further injected into the prompt-enhanced molecular representations through gated fusion, producing interaction-aware molecular embeddings for both drugs. Finally, the updated drug representations, their element-wise similarity and difference features, and the pair-level fragment interaction representation are concatenated and fed into a feed-forward classifier for multiclass DDI prediction.

Compared with conventional pairwise classifiers that directly concatenate two molecular vectors, the proposed method explicitly captures cross-drug fragment relations and preserves the pretrained chemical priors of MolFCL. Compared with hierarchical DDI models such as HDN-DDI, our method does not reconstruct a new atom--substructure--molecule encoder; instead, it introduces a lightweight and interpretable interaction module at the fine-tuning stage, making it more naturally compatible with molecular pretraining.
